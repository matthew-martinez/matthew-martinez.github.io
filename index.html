Background // Projects // LinkedIn

I am a social scientist based in the Washington, D.C. metro area. I am passionate about social science research methods, data science (in addition to other computational approaches to social research), and spatial analysis. I have expertise in human subjects research; statistical programming; survey and experimental design; data analysis and visualization; geospatial analysis; and social network analysis.

I received my Ph.D. in Anthropology from the University of Nevada, Las Vegas where I focused on the emergence and stabilization of social norms and human cooperation and collective action. My dissertation took a mechanistic approach to understanding the influence of social organization, norms, and values (i.e., culture) on social, political, and economic attitudes and behaviors.

Currently, I am a Research Associate working at Population Reference Bureau in the U.S. Programs department/a>. My research at PRB focuses on demographic, social, and economic patterns and trends and spatial demography within the United States. I also play an integral role in enhancing data visualization capabilities across the organization.

I am proficient in several programming languages and software packages for statistical and scientific computing:

  • R
  • Python
  • SAS
  • Stata

I also have experience in multiple GIS software packages:

  • ArcGIS (Pro and ArcMap)
  • QGIS
  • MapInfo

More recently, I have become interested in web development and data visualization for communicating and disseminating research results. I am currently learning:

  • Linux/Unix
  • HTML
  • CSS
  • JavaScript
