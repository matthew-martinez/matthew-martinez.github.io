Matthew Martinez, Ph.D.


I am a social science research methodologist based in the Washington, D.C. metro area. I am passionate about research methods, data science, and programming. I have expertise in human subjects research; statistical programming; survey and experimental design; data analysis and visualization; geospatial analysis; and social network analysis.

I received my Ph.D. in Anthropology from the University of Nevada, Las Vegas where I focused on the emergence and stabilization of social norms, cooperation, and collective action. My dissertation took a mechanistic approach to understanding the influence of social organization, norms, and values (i.e., culture) on social, political, and economic attitudes and behaviors.

Currently, I am a Research Associate at PRB in the U.S. Programs department. My research focuses on demographic, social, and economic patterns and trends in the United States. I also play an integral role in enhancing data visualization capabilities across the organization.

I am a proponent of open source, open science, and open data. I am proficient in several programming languages and software packages for statistical and scientific computing:

  • R
  • Python
  • SAS
  • Stata

And multiple GIS software packages:

  • ArcGIS (Pro and ArcMap)
  • QGIS
  • MapInfo

Continuously learning new things is important to me. Lately, I have been learning:

  • Linux/Unix
  • HTML & CSS
  • JavaScript

Take a look at some of my work on the Projects page or my GitHub.
